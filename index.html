THE ARCHITECTURAL VISION

Suspended Between Pacific Tides and Coastal Sky

Villa Solis represents a rare dialogue between brutalist integrity and coastal softness. The architecture utilizes sweeping cast-concrete structures that anchor deep into Malibu's shoreline granite, allowing cantilevered glass pavilions to float weightlessly above the surf.

Every room has been structured to capture the kinetic transition of Pacific light. Here, interior space dissolves completely into exterior vista, offering a lifestyle centered around organic rhythm, architectural silence, and raw coastal power.

Designed by Studio Ascend

Technical Manifesto

A Masterclass in Materiality & Design Engineering

Every line, concrete pour, and custom steel fixture has been precision-designed to create a structure that is both emotionally grounding and structurally invulnerable.

Architectural Geometry

Designed by famed modernist architect Marcus Vance, the structure relies on a cantilevered structural slab anchored directly into Malibu coastal granite. The result is a series of floating terraces that project over the shoreline, maximizing panoramic Pacific views.
